We are going to look at the example of a card game (a simplified version of blackjack), which will introduce many of the concepts that you need to learn. You will also consider how the design of the game might be approached using the procedural paradigm so that you can compare and contrast the two approaches.

A simple card game - blackjack

The game is played by two players with a deck of 52 cards. Cards are worth their face value, except for the ace, which is worth 1 or 11 points (the player can choose). Court cards (jack, queen, and king) are worth 10 points each. A hand's value is the sum of the values of the cards. The player will play against the computer (an AI system that will be referred to as Alana). The aim of the game is to have the hand with the highest value, up to a value of 21. The cards will be shuffled and the player and Alana will be dealt two cards each. The player's cards will be shown face up. One of Alana's cards will be displayed face up, and the other will be face down.

If either the player or Alana has an ace and a ten, or an ace and a court card (hand with a value of 21), they have immediately won the round. If both players have an initial hand with a value of 21, the game is drawn.

If either the player or Alana has an ace and a ten, or an ace and a court card (hand with a value of 21), they have immediately won the round. If both players have an initial hand with a value of 21, the game is drawn.

Otherwise, the player can take one of two actions: they can 'stick' (which means that they don't want any more cards) or they can 'draw' (which means that they will be dealt another card). If the value of the player's hand exceeds 21, the hand is 'bust', and the player has lost the game.

Once the player has decided to 'stick', Alana's face-down card will be revealed, and Alana can draw more cards, one at a time. Alana will 'decide' each time whether to stick or take another card. If the hand's value exceeds 21, the hand is 'bust', and Alana has lost the game.

If both hands have a value of less than 21, the hand with the highest value wins the game.

A win is worth 2 points and a draw is worth 1 point. The first player to reach 20 points is the overall winner.
